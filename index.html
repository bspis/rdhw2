Our Solar System

Our solar system is a magnificent place, and one of the few places in the known universe where conditions allowed for the evolution of life as we know it. We have two kinds of planets in our solar system: rocky planets and dwarf planets (such as Mercury, Venus, Earth, Ceres, Pluto, Eris, Makemake, Haumea, & other Kuiper Belt objects) and gas giants (such as Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, & Neptune).

Life as we know it can only thrive in certain conditions. Gas giants, which are mostly composed of helium and/or hydrogen, are not known to support life at all. Meanwhile, planets like Mercury and Venus, close as they are to the sun, would be too hot to support most known life forms, while dwarf planets in the asteroid belt and Kuiper Belt (the largest & most well-known of these being Pluto) are too far from the sun. How lucky we are, then to have Earth — a miraculous planet, right in the sweet spot to provide efficient sunlight for photosynthesis!
